Stockholm Chamber Brass

Stockholm Chamber Brass regularly perform around the world, playing in major festivals such as the Bad Kissingen Sommer, Schleswig-Holstein and Niedesächsische Musiktage.

To date, the ensemble has recorded 6 critically acclaimed CDs, The American Record Guide wrote of the album Clockworks, “I cannot imagine that a better brass quintet has ever existed.”

Alongside creating a legacy of repertoire for Brass Quintet, the group are passionate educators and regularly give masterclasses to the next generation of brass musicians. For over 30 years Stockholm Chamber Brass has helped develop brass quintet playing to the highest level around the world and they are now looking to help inspire both the next generation of brass ensembles and the next generation of composers. For the past 6 years the group has run the Stockholm Chamber Brass Academy in idyllic Leksand, Sweden. More information on the course, and how to apply, can be found here.

The Last Post

Towards the close of WW2, Army signaller Dennis Marshall wrote a series of moving letters to his fiancée, tracing his journey and his experiences. Inspired by the Last Post bugle call, the work is a very personal exploration of war and memory in an intimate setting, where audiences can experience the letters together.

WorldBrass

Since it's founding in 1995 international brass ensemble WorldBrass has developed into one of the most famous ensembles of its kind in Germany.

The unique quality of WorldBrass is the shared understanding of the ensemble. There are no fixed positions within the group, nor is there an outstanding “star”. However, one principle runs through every program: WorldBrass wants to entertain its audience at a high level.
